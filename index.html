Why you're taking it
Estradiol is your primary female hormone. As perimenopause progresses, your ovaries produce less. This patch delivers a steady dose through your skin — bypassing the liver processing that oral estrogen requires. At 50mcg it's a moderate starting dose designed to reduce symptoms and protect your bones, brain, heart, and vaginal tissue long-term.
What to expect week by week
Weeks 1–2: Possible breast tenderness, bloating, mild skin sensitivity at patch site. Normal as your body adjusts.

Weeks 2–4: Better sleep, reduced hot flashes, improved mood and mental clarity. Skin starts to improve.

Month 2–3: Full symptom relief. Stable energy. Long-term bone and cardiovascular protection begins.
Side effects to track
Common early on (usually resolve): Breast tenderness · bloating · mild nausea · skin irritation at site · mild headaches

Contact NP Daphne if: Severe headache · vision changes · chest pain · shortness of breath · leg swelling
How to apply
✓ Change day: every Sunday — set a weekly alarm right now
✓ Rotate sites: lower abdomen → right hip → left hip → upper buttock. Never same spot within 4 weeks
✓ Clean dry skin — wash, rinse, dry, wait 5 min
✓ Press firmly for 10 seconds, smooth all edges
✓ Never on breasts or irritated skin or waistband
✓ If peeling: press edge back, secure with medical tape — don't replace early
✓ Remove: fold sticky sides together, bin it — not the toilet

Why you're taking it
Women need testosterone too. It's essential for energy, libido, motivation, muscle tone, bone density, and cognitive function. Levels decline in perimenopause alongside estrogen — low testosterone is often behind the "flat" feeling, low drive, mental fog, loss of strength. At 0.50mg/ml this is a low physiological dose restoring what's naturally declining, not pushing above normal female range.
What to expect
Weeks 1–3: Subtle shift. Slightly more motivated or energetic. Libido changes take 4–6 weeks.

Month 2: Improved drive, better response to training, faster recovery, mental clarity.

Month 3+: Full effect on libido, mood stability, muscle tone, sustained energy.
Side effects to track
At this low dose, uncommon — but track: Increased acne or oily skin · slight facial hair · voice changes (rare at this dose) · irritability (usually means dose too high)

Track daily in journal: Energy and motivation — helps calibrate whether the dose is working
How to apply
✓ Morning only — consistent time = stable blood levels
✓ Step 1: Apply cream to inner wrists
✓ Step 2: Use wrists to apply to outer/inner thighs
✓ Let dry 2–3 min before dressing
✓ Wash hands after — don't transfer to others
✓ Days 1–21 only — stop day 22, resume day 1 of next cycle

Why you're taking it
Progesterone balances estrogen. Using estrogen alone (if you have a uterus) increases risk of uterine lining overgrowth. Progesterone protects the uterus and has its own benefits: better sleep, reduced anxiety, anti-inflammatory effects. The SR (slow release) compounded form sustains levels through the night — which is why bedtime specifically.
What to expect
First few nights: Slightly sleepy or relaxed after taking it — that's it working.

Days 14–28 ongoing: Better sleep during second half of cycle. Reduced PMS anxiety. Less severe cramping over time.

When you stop (day 29+): Sleep may feel lighter for 1–2 days as it clears. Normal.
Side effects to track
Common: Drowsiness at bedtime (expected) · slight breast tenderness · bloating first cycle

Contact NP Daphne if: Severe dizziness · depression · significant breast pain · unusual breakthrough bleeding
How to take
✓ Bedtime only — SR releases overnight
✓ Swallow whole with full glass of water
✓ Days 14–28 of cycle only
✓ Current cycle started Apr 23 — Day 14 = May 6, Day 28 = ~May 20
✓ New cycle ~May 23 → progesterone resumes ~June 6 (Day 14 new cycle)
✓ If you forget: skip that night, resume next — never double up
