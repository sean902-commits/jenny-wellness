Why you're taking it
Estradiol is your primary female hormone. As you move through perimenopause, your ovaries produce less of it. This patch delivers a steady, consistent dose through your skin — avoiding the liver processing that oral estrogen requires. At 50mcg it's a moderate starting dose designed to reduce symptoms and protect your bones, brain, heart, and vaginal tissue long-term.
What to expect
Weeks 1–2: Possible breast tenderness, bloating, or slight skin sensitivity at patch site. These are normal as your body adjusts.

Weeks 2–4: Better sleep, reduced hot flashes, improved mood and mental clarity. Skin may start feeling better. Vaginal dryness begins to improve.

Month 2–3: Full effect on symptoms. Energy more stable. Improved bone protection begins long-term.
Potential side effects to watch
Common early on (usually resolve): Breast tenderness · bloating · slight nausea · skin irritation at patch site · mild headaches

Track in your journal: Any spotting or breakthrough bleeding · persistent headaches · breast changes · mood changes

Contact NP Daphne if: Severe headache · vision changes · chest pain · shortness of breath · leg swelling
How to apply
✓ Change day: every Sunday — set a weekly alarm
✓ Rotate sites: lower abdomen → right hip → left hip → upper buttock. Never reuse a site within 4 weeks
✓ Clean skin: wash, rinse, dry completely — wait 5 min before applying
✓ Press firmly for 10 seconds, smooth all edges down
✓ Never on breasts or irritated/broken skin or waistbands
✓ If peeling: press edge back down or secure with medical tape. Don't replace early.
✓ Removing: fold sticky sides together, put in the bin — not the toilet

Why you're taking it
Women need testosterone too — it's not just a male hormone. It's essential for energy, libido, motivation, muscle tone, bone density, and cognitive function. Levels decline in perimenopause alongside estrogen, and low testosterone is often behind the "flat" feeling — low drive, mental fog, loss of strength. At 0.50mg/ml this is a low physiological dose designed to restore what's naturally declining, not push above normal range.
What to expect
Weeks 1–3: Subtle. May feel slightly more motivated or energetic. Libido changes can take 4–6 weeks to notice.

Month 2: Improved energy and drive. Better response to strength training — muscle recovery faster. Mental clarity improvements.

Month 3+: Full effect on libido, mood stability, muscle tone, and sustained energy throughout the day.
Potential side effects to watch
At this low dose, side effects are uncommon but track: Increased acne or oily skin · slight facial hair growth · voice changes (rare at this dose) · irritability or aggression (usually means dose too high)

Track in your journal: Energy and motivation daily — helps calibrate whether the dose is right
How to apply
✓ Morning only — consistent time builds stable blood levels
✓ Step 1: Apply cream to inner wrists
✓ Step 2: Use wrists to apply to outer/inner thighs
✓ Let dry 2–3 min before dressing
✓ Wash hands after — don't transfer to others (especially children)
✓ Days 1–21 only — stop day 22, resume day 1 of next cycle

Why you're taking it
Progesterone balances estrogen. Using estrogen alone without progesterone (if you have a uterus) increases risk of uterine lining overgrowth. Progesterone protects the uterus and has its own benefits: better sleep, reduced anxiety, and anti-inflammatory effects. The SR (slow release) compounded form is designed to sustain levels through the night — which is why it's taken at bedtime specifically. It genuinely helps with sleep quality.
What to expect
First few nights: You may feel slightly sleepy or relaxed after taking it — that's it working. Don't fight it.

Days 14–28 ongoing: Better sleep quality during the second half of your cycle. Reduced PMS anxiety and irritability. Less severe cramping over time.

When you stop (day 28+): Sleep may feel lighter for a couple of days as it clears. Normal.
Potential side effects to watch
Common: Drowsiness after taking (expected and fine at bedtime) · slight breast tenderness · bloating in the first cycle

Track: Sleep quality on nights you take it vs nights you don't — the difference should be noticeable after 2–3 weeks

Contact NP Daphne if: Severe dizziness · depression · significant breast pain · breakthrough bleeding that seems unusual
